Category
FATAL
Fatality: A death directly resulting from a work injury regardless of the length of the time between the injury and death.
PTD
Permanent Total Disability - a work injury which incapacitates an employee permanently and results in termination of employment on medical grounds (e.g. loss of limb(s)) permanent brain damage, loss of sight) and predudes the individual from working eighter at sea or ashore
PPD
Permanent Partial Disability - any work injury which results in the complete loss, or permanent loss of use, of any member or part of the body, or any pre-existing disability of the injured member or impaired body function, that partially restricts or limits an employee basis to work on permanent basis at sea. Such an individual could be employed ashore but not at sea in line with industry guidelines.
LWC
Lost Workday Case - an injury which results in an individual being unable to work on a scheduled work shift on the day following the injury unless caused by delays in getting medical treatment ashore.
RWC
Restricted Work Case - an injury which results in an individual being unable to perform all normally assigned work functions during a scheduled work shift or being assigned to another job on a temporary or permanent basis on the day following the injury.
MTC
Medical Treatment Case - any work - related loss of consiousness (unless due to ill health), injury or illness requiring more than first aid treatment by a physician, dentist, surgeon, or registered medical personnel, e.g. nurse or paramedic under the standing orders of a physician, or under the specific order of a physician or if at sea with no physician onboard could be considered as being in the province of a physician.
FAC
First Aid Case - any work related accident resulting in minor injury requiring on board first aid for cuts, bruises, abrasions, etc., where medical assistance from shore is not required
